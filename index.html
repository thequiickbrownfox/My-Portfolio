About

A slightly more human summary than a CV bullet list.

A data journalist by chance, a data scientist by choice. My route into machine learning has been somewhat unconventional, but in hindsight, everything aligns. I began by studying political philosophy, where my dissertation explored questions around digital realities and the metaverse. That curiosity later took a more empirical form through data journalism, working with political and economic datasets, and eventually led me to formal training in data science.

Across different fields, the common thread has remained the same: I enjoy making sense of complex data and uncovering patterns that are not immediately obvious.

Outside datasets, I spend time watching films, occasionally overanalysing thriller series, and currently attempting to convince my ukulele that I know what I’m doing.
